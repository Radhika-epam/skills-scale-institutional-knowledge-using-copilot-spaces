# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Collaboration
- Works closely with UX/UI Designers on implementation details
- Coordinates with DevOps Engineers on deployment and infrastructure needs
- Partners with Business Analysts on requirement clarification
- Engages with Security Champion on security requirements and code reviews
- Supports QA and Support/Operations teams during testing and release phases

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Collaboration
- Partners with Business Analysts on requirements refinement
- Aligns with UX/UI Designers on user needs and accessibility
- Consults with Security Champion on compliance and security priorities
- Works with Support/Operations Lead to understand operational constraints and support feedback

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Collaboration
- Escalates blockers from DevOps Engineer or Security Champion
- Coordinates dependencies across Developers, Business Analysts, and UX/UI Designers
- Ensures Support/Operations Lead is included in planning for operational impact
- Manages cross-team communication and removes impediments

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create intuitive, accessible user interfaces and experiences in collaboration with Product and Developers. They ensure solutions meet customer needs and usability standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design user interfaces and user experiences that meet accessibility standards
- Conduct user research and validate design decisions with stakeholders
- Collaborate with Developers on design feasibility and implementation details
- Participate in backlog refinement and acceptance criteria definition
- Review and iterate on designs based on feedback and testing

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Ensure consistency across product surfaces
- Reduce user friction and support burden through good design
- Enable teams to build features that users love

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- Collaboration on user stories and acceptance criteria
- Design system documentation and component specifications
- Participation in sprint planning and demos

### Collaboration
- Works with Product Manager to understand user needs and business goals
- Partners with Developers on implementation feasibility and technical constraints
- Coordinates with Business Analyst on requirement validation
- Aligns with Support/Operations Lead on user feedback and pain points

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers own CI/CD pipeline health, infrastructure reliability, and deployment automation. They enable teams to deploy frequently and safely while maintaining system stability.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automated testing infrastructure
- Manage cloud resources, environments (dev, staging, production), and infrastructure as code
- Automate deployment processes and reduce manual deployment steps
- Monitor system health, performance, and reliability
- Escalate deployment blockers and collaborate on release readiness

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize downtime and production incidents
- Reduce deployment risk through automation and observability
- Support team velocity through frictionless infrastructure

### Typical Communication
- Release coordination and deployment scheduling
- Infrastructure and environment documentation
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Collaboration on build failures and deployment blockers

### Collaboration
- Partners with Developers on build automation and environment setup
- Coordinates with Project Manager on deployment windows and release schedules
- Works with Security Champion on infrastructure security and compliance
- Supports Support/Operations Lead on runbook automation and incident response

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge stakeholder requirements and delivery teams. They document, validate, and refine acceptance criteria to ensure solutions meet business needs and are testable.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document requirements from stakeholders
- Define and validate acceptance criteria in user stories
- Clarify ambiguities and scope with Product Manager and stakeholders
- Support backlog prioritization and impact analysis
- Identify risks, dependencies, and cross-team handoffs

### Goals
- Ensure clear, testable requirements that reduce rework
- Improve communication between stakeholders and delivery teams
- Minimize scope creep and misaligned deliverables
- Support faster, more accurate sprint planning and estimation

### Typical Communication
- User story documentation and refinement
- Requirements clarification meetings with stakeholders
- Collaboration on acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Risk and dependency identification in planning

### Collaboration
- Works closely with Product Manager on requirement prioritization
- Collaborates with Developers on technical feasibility and design
- Aligns with UX/UI Designer on user experience requirements
- Partners with QA on test planning and coverage
- Coordinates with Support/Operations Lead on operational requirements

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
Security Champions ensure security best practices are integrated throughout development and release. They act as security advocates, conduct reviews, and support incident response communication.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct threat modeling and security reviews on features and architecture
- Integrate security requirements into acceptance criteria
- Review code and infrastructure for security vulnerabilities
- Support incident response and communication during security events
- Drive security awareness and continuous improvement across the team

### Goals
- Embed security into development from the start
- Reduce security vulnerabilities and compliance risk
- Enable fast, secure delivery without sacrificing agility
- Foster a culture of shared security responsibility

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews and threat modeling sessions
- Code review comments and security feedback
- Documentation of security requirements and best practices
- Incident response communication and post-mortem facilitation

### Collaboration
- Partners with Developers on secure coding practices and code reviews
- Aligns with Product Manager on security priorities and trade-offs
- Works with DevOps Engineer on infrastructure security and compliance
- Collaborates with Business Analyst on security-related acceptance criteria
- Supports Project Manager on security risk escalation

---

## Support/Operations Lead

### Role Summary
Support/Operations Leads coordinate post-release support, operational runbooks, and continuous improvement. They ensure learnings from incidents and user feedback inform product decisions and operational excellence.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate post-release support and issue triage
- Document operational runbooks and troubleshooting procedures
- Collect and communicate customer feedback and operational pain points
- Participate in incident response and post-mortem retrospectives
- Identify operational risks and requirements during planning

### Goals
- Ensure smooth post-release operations and user support
- Reduce operational incidents and customer escalations
- Drive continuous improvement based on operational data
- Enable fast, confident releases through readiness planning

### Typical Communication
- Operational runbooks and troubleshooting guides
- Post-release monitoring and incident reports
- Feedback to product and engineering teams
- Participation in planning and retrospectives

### Collaboration
- Works with Product Manager on feature usability and support impact
- Aligns with Developers on operational requirements and debugging
- Partners with DevOps Engineer on infrastructure monitoring and incident response
- Collaborates with UX/UI Designer on reducing user confusion and support burden
- Supports Project Manager on release readiness and operational risk

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When planning projects, ensure all relevant personas are included in kickoff meetings and communication planning.
- Use collaboration patterns to identify handoffs and dependencies during project planning and execution.
